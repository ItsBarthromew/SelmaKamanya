Beyond the Crown

"She didn't enter the room to be seen. She entered to build."

Selma Kamanya was born and raised in Namibia, a country she has carried in her heart across every stage, boardroom, and international platform she has ever stepped onto. From a young age, she understood something most people take years to learn: that your presence is your most powerful asset, and that where you come from does not limit where you are going.

Her journey into pageantry was never about the crown. It was about the platform. When she entered Miss Namibia in 2018, she was already thinking beyond the stage, asking herself what this visibility could open, who it could reach, and what it could build. She understood that beauty pageants, at their highest level, are not about beauty at all. They are about representation, diplomacy, and the power of a face and a voice that a nation can rally behind.

That understanding has shaped everything she has done since.

Winning Miss World Namibia in 2025 was not the destination. It was the loudest door that has ever opened for her and she walked through it with a blueprint already in hand.
Today Selma operates at the intersection of beauty, business, and nation building. She is the founder and driving force behind GN1, a platform connecting Namibian talent to global opportunities and cultural conversations. She is an investor in real estate through Okuyambekwa Properties, building a vision of economic empowerment through property ownership. She is a sought after speaker on stages across Africa and beyond, carrying conversations about identity, leadership, women in business, and what it truly means to position an emerging nation on the world stage.
But more than any title or venture, Selma Kamanya is a builder. She builds platforms. She builds visibility. She builds futures for herself, for her partners, and for the young Namibians watching her who need to see that it is possible.

Investing in the Next Generation

Through the Global Nation Academy, Selma is doing the work that matters most to her by equipping young Namibians with the tools, knowledge, and exposure they need to compete on a global stage. Financial literacy. Leadership development. Global positioning. The academy is her long term investment in a Namibia that owns its future.
